About Me

I am a third-year Computer Science student with a passion for software development, continuously seeking to expand my knowledge through research and personal projects. Over the past three years, I've gained experience across various programming domains, with a particular focus on web development, which I've dedicated the most time to and feel most comfortable with.

Before enrolling in university, I completed an accredited course in Front-End Development, which played a significant role in sparking my interest in the field. It helped me grasp the basics and gave me a new perspective on the software development industry. After the course, I continued learning new skills on my own, which became much easier as my foundation grew stronger.

During university, I've expanded my knowledge further, gaining a solid understanding of .NET and C#. This was a refreshing experience, especially after working with C++ in high school and the first year of my degree. I also enjoyed learning Java, which felt quite similar to C#, and through both languages, I developed a strong grasp of Object-Oriented Programming (OOP). I even explored GUI development using XAML in C# and JavaFX in Java.

In addition to these skills, I have also learned Python and created APIs using Flask. I have become proficient in working with relational databases through MySQL Workbench. This diverse set of experiences has given me a solid foundation in software development, and I am eager to continue applying and expanding these skills through both academic and personal projects.
